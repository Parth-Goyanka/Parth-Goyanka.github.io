<div class="section-header">
  <h2>Reading List</h2>
  <p>Books and papers that have shaped my understanding of control and power systems.</p>
</div>

<section class="section">
  <h3>Currently Reading</h3>
  <div class="reading-grid">
    <div class="book-card glass-card">
      <div class="book-icon"><i class="fas fa-book-open"></i></div>
      <h3>Nonlinear Systems</h3>
      <p class="book-author">Hassan K. Khalil</p>
      <p class="book-note">The definitive text on nonlinear systems analysis. Currently working through Chapter 4 on Lyapunov stability and its applications to feedback stabilization.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="book-card glass-card">
      <div class="book-icon"><i class="fas fa-bolt"></i></div>
      <h3>Power System Stability and Control</h3>
      <p class="book-author">Prabha Kundur</p>
      <p class="book-note">Comprehensive treatment of power system dynamics. Focusing on voltage stability mechanisms and their relationship to load characteristics.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="section">
  <h3>Completed</h3>
  <div class="reading-grid">
    <div class="book-card glass-card">
      <div class="book-icon"><i class="fas fa-check-circle"></i></div>
      <h3>Optimal Control Theory</h3>
      <p class="book-author">Donald E. Kirk</p>
      <p class="book-note">Excellent introduction to variational methods, Pontryagin's maximum principle, and dynamic programming. The examples are particularly well-chosen.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="book-card glass-card">
      <div class="book-icon"><i class="fas fa-check-circle"></i></div>
      <h3>Linear System Theory and Design</h3>
      <p class="book-author">Chi-Tsong Chen</p>
      <p class="book-note">Clear exposition of state-space methods, controllability, observability, and realization theory. Great foundation for advanced control topics.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>
